# SpendSmart — Sprout

A Behavioral Finance App for Gen-Z Students

## Overview

SpendSmart — Sprout is a minimal, user-centered finance application designed specifically for Gen-Z students who struggle with overspending and inconsistent saving habits. Unlike traditional finance apps that focus only on tracking expenses, this platform addresses the **emotional and behavioral drivers behind financial decisions**.

The goal of this project is to move beyond numbers and create a system that builds **self-awareness, discipline, and sustainable financial habits**.

Live Demo: https://smart-spend-indol.vercel.app/

---

## Problem Statement

Most personal finance apps are:

* Data-heavy and overwhelming
* Focused only on expense tracking
* Not tailored to student behavior patterns

As a result, users:

* Ignore insights
* Fail to build consistent saving habits
* Lack emotional awareness of spending decisions

---

## Solution

SpendSmart introduces a **behavior-first financial system** that combines tracking with habit formation and emotional awareness.

The platform simplifies financial management while encouraging **intentional spending and structured saving**.

---

## Key Features

### 1. Money Journal

* Allows users to tag expenses with emotions
* Builds awareness of spending triggers
* Encourages reflective financial behavior

### 2. Vault (Goal-Locked Savings)

* Users can lock money toward specific goals
* Prevents impulsive withdrawals
* Reinforces disciplined saving

### 3. Gamified Habit System

* Tracks consistency in saving and spending behavior
* Encourages positive habits through simple reinforcement mechanisms

---

## Design Approach

* Focused on **minimal UI** to reduce cognitive overload
* Designed for **quick interactions and clarity**
* Prioritized **behavioral psychology over raw data visualization**
* Ensured mobile-first responsiveness for student usage patterns

---

## Research & Analysis

* Conducted competitive analysis of:

  * Walnut
  * YNAB
  * Splitwise

### Key Insight:

Existing apps fail to combine:

* Emotional awareness
* Goal-based saving
* Habit tracking

This revealed a clear opportunity to design a **student-centric behavioral finance product**.

## Usability Testing & Results

* 91% task completion rate across 8 core screens
* 64% of users reported improved awareness of spending behavior
* Users found the Money Journal feature most impactful
